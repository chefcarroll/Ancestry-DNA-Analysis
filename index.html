      The Sampling process

      1. Sample Collection

      Individuals provide a DNA sample, typically through a saliva sample or a cheek swab. The sample contains cells with DNA, and the goal is to extract and analyze the genetic material within these cells.

      2. DNA Extraction

      The DNA is extracted from the collected sample using various laboratory techniques. This process separates the DNA from other components in the sample.

      3. DNA Sequencing

      Modern ancestry DNA testing often involves DNA sequencing. This can be done using various methods, such as microarray-based genotyping or next-generation sequencing. The sequencing process reads the order of nucleotide bases (adenine, thymine, cytosine, and guanine) in an individual's DNA.

      4. Data Analysis

      The raw DNA data is then analyzed to identify specific markers or variations in the DNA sequence. Ancestry testing companies compare these markers to reference databases that include genetic information from diverse populations around the world.

      5. Ancestral Inference

      By comparing an individual's DNA markers to those of known populations, the testing company can make predictions about the individual's likely ancestral origins. This process is based on the assumption that certain genetic markers are associated with specific geographic regions due to historical migration patterns and population movements.
